Jacob Pride, PhD

English Instructor at Manhattan Christian College and Religion Teacher at St. Xavier Catholic School

I live in Manhattan, KS with my wife, Caitlin, and my two boys, William and Charles, and our first girl, Elsie Grace! I joined the pre-theologate program my second semester at Ave Maria University and was a member of AMU's first graduating class at the new campus in 2008. I received the St. Thomas Aquinas Award for Balance and Eutropalia (probably because I am an Eagle Scout and play the ukulele), and after a year at Mount St. Mary’s seminary, I served as a FOCUS missionary for two years and met Caitlin. We dated while I earned a master’s in English at Georgia Southern University. My AMU thesis advisor encouraged me to continue my education and get a PhD in English at the University of Denver. Caitlin and I were married in 2013, and I received my doctorate in 2017. My dissertation concerns Catholic literary theory and Southern Gothic Literature. I taught English at Kansas State University, and I now teach theology at St. Francis Xavier High School and English night classes at Manhattan Christian College.
